Fresh Start in Education

MAT and Private Governing-Organisation Commercial Architecture

A Salesforce and MCAE architecture that represented schools within their wider Multi-Academy Trust, independent school group and private governing-organisation relationships, then used purchases within connected organisations as a commercial signal for marketing qualification and sales routing.

Salesforce & CRM Automation Data & Analytics Commercial Strategy Marketing
Project overview

Advocated for, designed and implemented an architecture that mapped school Accounts to their governing organisations, aligned related Accounts, Contacts and Leads through Custom Metadata Type-driven automation and domain matching, and used purchases within connected organisations to trigger Salesforce Campaigns, MCAE validation, grading, nurture and automated sales handoff.

Key result Mapped approximately 50% of school Accounts to 215 MATs and private governing organisations.

01

The commercial problem

A substantial proportion of school accounts did not operate as fully independent organisations. Approximately half belonged to Multi-Academy Trusts, independent school groups or other private governing organisations, while every school also operated within a local-authority context.

The company historically risked approaching schools as isolated buying entities, limiting visibility of shared governance, related purchases, buying momentum and influence across connected organisations. Salesforce did not contain a complete, governed structure for these relationships, and Leads could not be directly linked to Accounts before conversion.

  • Approximately half of school accounts belonged to a private governing organisation
  • Risk of treating connected schools as isolated buying entities
  • No standard Lead-to-Account lookup before conversion
  • No process for acting on purchases made elsewhere in the same organisation

02

Mapping the governing-organisation landscape

Identified the governing-organisation membership of school accounts across Multi-Academy Trusts, independent school groups and other private governing organisations, then segmented the entire school-account database according to the correct governing organisation or confirmed lack of membership.

Represented each governing organisation through the additional parent-account relationship within the Salesforce account model, allowing school accounts to retain both local-authority context and private governing-organisation context at the same time.

  • Approximately 215 private governing organisations
  • Approximately 50% of school accounts mapped
  • Additional parent-account relationships
  • Dual local-authority and private-governance context

03

Lookup keys and metadata-driven alignment

Used Custom Metadata Types to support repeatable assignment logic across accounts, contacts and leads sharing the same MAT or governing organisation, and built Salesforce automation to align related records with the appropriate master or MAT account.

Added custom fields that created lookup keys for governing-organisation assignment, enabling unconverted Leads to retain organisational context despite there being no standard direct Lead-to-Account lookup before conversion. Validation rules protected the completeness and accuracy of the resulting hierarchy.

  • Custom Metadata Type-driven Flows
  • Custom lookup-key fields for Leads
  • Account, Contact and Lead alignment automation
  • Validation rules protecting hierarchy integrity

04

One dynamic, multi-object Screen Flow

Designed and implemented a single dynamic Salesforce Screen Flow that adapted its behaviour according to the object and record context from which it was launched. From a school Account it allowed alignment to the correct private governing organisation, while from a local-authority or local-authority department Account it prevented an inappropriate alignment being created.

From a Lead, the Flow presented the same user experience while populating different Lead-specific fields in the background, and from a MAT Account it displayed a summary of related Accounts and Leads, with safeguards against selecting similarly named MATs and support for requesting a new MAT Account where none existed.

  • School Account alignment path
  • Local-authority Account exclusion path
  • Lead-specific field population path
  • MAT Account related-record summary path
  • Final review before submission

05

Domain matching for new records

Documented thousands of email domains associated with MATs, independent school groups and other private governing organisations, storing them as Custom Metadata Type reference data. Flow automation checked newly created Leads and Contacts not already within the architecture against these records, using exact like-for-like matching and wildcard matching to identify valid domain variations.

Government and personal email domains were excluded from this matching to prevent false MAT or independent-school assignments, and the Flow completed quietly when no valid match was found, avoiding unnecessary interruption or forced manual action.

  • Thousands of documented organisational domains
  • Exact and wildcard domain matching
  • Government and personal domain exclusions
  • Quiet completion on no match

06

Purchases as a tangential buying signal

Advocated for and designed the wider architecture around the principle that a purchase by one organisation could act as a tangential buying signal for related Accounts and Leads. When an Account within a MAT or private governing organisation entered purchase, related Accounts not currently purchasing and related Leads were added to Salesforce Campaigns synchronised into MCAE.

MCAE validated whether these related prospects were legitimate and appropriate to pursue, adjusted qualification according to buying momentum, placed validated prospects into follow-up journeys, and automatically routed qualified Leads and Accounts to the relevant sales teams.

  • Purchase-triggered Salesforce Campaigns
  • Salesforce-to-MCAE Campaign synchronisation
  • MCAE legitimacy validation and grading
  • Automated qualified-record sales routing

Outcomes

What the MAT and governing-organisation architecture enabled

Account-family visibility

Mapped approximately 50% of school accounts to 215 MATs and private governing organisations, moving away from treating schools as isolated buying entities.

Lead-to-organisation continuity

Custom lookup keys let unconverted Leads carry governing-organisation context despite no standard Lead-to-Account lookup existing before conversion.

One Flow, many contexts

A single dynamic Screen Flow supported school, local-authority, Lead and MAT Account contexts while preventing inappropriate alignments.

Automated sales handoff

Purchases within one part of a governing organisation triggered validated, graded and automatically routed opportunities for related accounts and leads.
