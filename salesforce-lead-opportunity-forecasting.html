Fresh Start in Education

Salesforce Lead Opportunity Forecasting and Automated Closure

A guided, pre-conversion Lead opportunity process that calculated forecast value from historical purchasing data, preserved every attempt for reporting, and closed inactive work automatically after 21 days.

Salesforce & CRM Automation Data & Analytics Commercial Strategy Operations
Project overview

Designed and implemented a Salesforce process that enabled the sales team to create and manage opportunity-style activity directly from Lead records before conversion, using a guided Screen Flow, dependent picklists, 12-month forecast-value modelling, a dedicated custom object and scheduled closure automation to prioritise higher-value activity and preserve repeated attempts.

Key result Used 12 months of purchasing data and automatically closed inactive opportunities after 21 days.

01

The commercial problem

The sales team wanted to create and manage opportunity-style activity for Leads before they were converted, but the Lead record did not provide a structured process for progressing, valuing, closing and retaining repeated sales attempts. A single Lead could experience several successful or unsuccessful opportunity cycles, creating a need to preserve historical outcomes without overcrowding the Lead record.

The team also needed a consistent way to measure how Leads were identified, contacted and progressed, prioritise opportunities with stronger historical revenue potential, and prevent inactive activity from remaining open indefinitely.

  • No structured pre-conversion opportunity process on the Lead record
  • Leads could carry several successful or unsuccessful attempts over time
  • Risk of overcrowding the Lead record with historical detail
  • No consistent way to prioritise higher-value activity

02

A guided, action-led Screen Flow

Once a Lead contained sufficient and permissible field information, a user could launch a Salesforce action from the Lead record that started a guided Screen Flow. The Flow activated a hidden checkbox marking the Lead opportunity as active, then guided the user through four sequential dependent picklists covering how the Lead was identified, how the organisation reached out, how the Lead responded, and the resulting outcome.

Each completed picklist unlocked the next stage, creating a controlled progression that enabled the sales team to record consistent, measurable information across identification, outreach, response and outcome.

  • Lead-readiness requirements before launch
  • Hidden activation checkbox
  • Identification, outreach, response and outcome picklists
  • Sequential unlocking of each stage

03

Forecast value from historical purchasing

When the Lead opportunity process began, Salesforce calculated a forecast value using purchasing data from the previous 12 months, based on organisation type, average rate, average weekly hours and average length of service in weeks. Historical rates were also checked against Multi-Academy Trust context, so the same organisation type could use different assumptions depending on MAT membership.

This allowed the sales team to focus on opportunities associated with organisation types that had historically generated more revenue, without treating the forecast as guaranteed income.

  • 12 months of historical purchasing data
  • Organisation type, rate, hours and service duration
  • MAT-adjusted rate assumptions
  • Sales prioritisation by forecast value

04

Preserving every opportunity attempt

Created a dedicated Salesforce custom object to store the results and progression of each Lead opportunity, generating a new custom-object record every time a Lead entered a new opportunity cycle. This preserved multiple successful and unsuccessful attempts against the same Lead while the custom-object record updated dynamically as fields were completed or changed.

Only the current Lead opportunity remained visible on the Lead record, with a link to the most recent related opportunity, while working fields were purged and refreshed for each new cycle and previous attempts stayed intact within the custom object.

  • Dedicated custom object per opportunity attempt
  • Dynamic record updates as fields changed
  • Current-only view on the Lead record
  • Lead-field purge and refresh between cycles

05

Automated closure of inactive opportunities

A dedicated last-updated timestamp refreshed whenever any of the four progression picklists changed. A scheduled Salesforce Flow monitored active Lead opportunities against this timestamp and automatically closed and deactivated any opportunity left without an update for 21 days.

Automated closure unlocked additional outcome options so the system-generated result could be stored alongside whatever the user had already documented, letting reports distinguish between user-recorded outcomes and those closed automatically through inactivity.

  • Last-updated timestamp on progression change
  • Scheduled Flow monitoring inactivity
  • 21-day automated closure and deactivation
  • User-recorded vs. automated outcome distinction

06

Reportable history and sales feedback

The combination of a dedicated custom object and consistent progression fields created a reportable history of repeated opportunity attempts, including attempts that did not result in a successful outcome. This let the sales team review compiled identification, outreach, response, forecast-value and outcome data across every cycle a Lead had been through.

The resulting dataset gave the sales team structured feedback that could be used to iterate and improve its approach, rather than relying on memory or informal notes about which Leads had already been worked and how.

  • Full history of successful and unsuccessful attempts
  • Compiled identification, outreach and response data
  • Forecast-value and outcome reporting
  • Structured feedback for sales-process improvement

Outcomes

What the forecasting and closure process enabled

Consistent, measurable progression

Four dependent picklists captured how each Lead was identified, contacted, responded and resolved, in a controlled and repeatable sequence.

Revenue-informed prioritisation

Forecast value from 12 months of purchasing data, adjusted for MAT context, helped the team focus on historically higher-value opportunities.

Clean records, full history

A dedicated custom object preserved every opportunity attempt while the Lead record stayed focused on the current cycle.

No opportunities left open indefinitely

Scheduled automation closed inactive opportunities after 21 days, distinguishing user-recorded outcomes from automated closures.
