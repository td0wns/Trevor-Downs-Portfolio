Local-Authority Account Hierarchy, Contract Pricing and Commercial Risk

A local-authority-centred Salesforce data model that represented local authorities as commercially influential parent accounts, connected contract pricing to Opportunities, and exposed where a single authority relationship could put many related accounts at risk.

01

The commercial problem

Every customer account, contact and lead operated within a geographic area overseen by a local authority, but authorities were commercially more significant than a standard geography field could represent. Depending on the area, an authority could set prices, fund services, commission the organisation, issue projects, restrict provider access, influence a buying committee, or remain a relatively passive observer.

Without a relational Salesforce model, the organisation could not consistently connect accounts with the authorities influencing them, segment communications by contract, or quantify authority-level exposure.

  • Authorities could set pricing, fund, commission or restrict service access.
  • Geography alone could not represent commercial and contractual influence.
  • Bespoke pricing could be overlooked within siloed Contract records.
  • No consolidated way to prioritise authority relationships by exposure.

02

A local-authority-centred account model

Advocated for representing each local authority as a Salesforce Account and assigning it as the parent account of every organisation within its geographic area, treating authorities as commercially influential stakeholders rather than only a geographic classification.

The model was applied across more than 1,000 Salesforce accounts and over 20,000 leads and contacts, representing approximately 240 local authorities.

  • Approximately 240 local-authority Accounts
  • 1,000+ related organisation Accounts
  • 20,000+ leads and contacts
  • Parent-account hierarchy across all Accounts

03

Metadata-driven assignment and governance

Built Salesforce Flows using Custom Metadata Types to support local-authority assignment and account ownership logic, making the rules easier to maintain than hard-coded automation. When an organisation received a local authority as its parent account, its contacts also received the relevant context for segmentation and marketing within MCAE, and leads carried the same context before conversion.

Validation rules ensured that only accounts explicitly marked as local authorities could be selected as parent accounts, and required every new organisation account to be assigned an appropriate local-authority parent on creation.

  • Custom Metadata Type-driven Flows
  • Account ownership assignment logic
  • Contact and Lead local-authority attribution
  • Parent-account eligibility validation rules
  • Mandatory parent validation on account creation

04

Contract-based segmentation

The hierarchy enabled segmentation by local-authority contract and commercial relationship rather than relying on geography alone, so bespoke messaging could reach organisations affected by a particular authority's pricing, contracts, commissioning requirements or service restrictions.

This created a structure through which the organisation could assess the number and commercial importance of accounts influenced by each local authority, supporting prioritisation of authority relationships according to their direct role and their wider influence over related accounts.

  • Contract- and authority-based MCAE segmentation
  • Bespoke messaging by pricing or commissioning context
  • Authority-level influence analysis
  • Relationship prioritisation by commercial exposure

05

Commercial concentration risk

The hierarchy highlighted commercial concentration risk where a weak relationship with one local authority could affect many otherwise independent accounts within its area. Oxfordshire illustrated this: the authority applied custom pricing and was otherwise relatively uninvolved, but the organisation's strong local popularity meant that deterioration in the authority relationship could create substantial exposure across accounts in the area.

Surfacing this kind of exposure gave the organisation a clearer basis for deciding which authority relationships most needed active management.

  • Authority-level exposure across related accounts
  • Oxfordshire custom-pricing example
  • Relationship-risk visibility for account planning

06

Contract pricing and Opportunity automation

Extended the Salesforce Contract object to store bespoke local-authority and contract-specific pricing, then built Flows that pulled the applicable contract price into Opportunities. This helped sales teams identify and use the correct price when preparing commercial discussions and quotations.

Pricing management spanned approximately 240 local authorities and roughly half that number of distinct contractual arrangements, improving forecasting accuracy by ensuring Opportunity values reflected the relevant contract rather than generic or manually remembered pricing.

  • Extended Contract object with bespoke pricing
  • Automated Opportunity pricing Flows
  • Sales quoting support
  • Improved revenue forecasting accuracy
