Salesforce Multi-Touch Attribution Model

A modular Salesforce attribution framework designed to show how marketing, sales, events, referrals and organisational relationships contributed to commercial outcomes across complex buying journeys.

Salesforce & CRM Automation Data & Analytics Commercial Strategy
Project overview

The framework replaced a single-touch interpretation of conversion with a structured model capable of recognising several meaningful interactions. Applicable channels received a weighted share of influence, with the final attribution normalised to 100%.

Key result Normalised applicable channel influence to 100%.

01

The commercial challenge

Commercial outcomes were rarely caused by one interaction. A prospect might attend an event, receive several campaign communications, speak with the sales team and be influenced by an existing organisational relationship before progressing.

Traditional first-touch or last-touch reporting could not represent this journey accurately. It risked overvaluing the most visible interaction while ignoring the wider combination of activity that contributed to the decision.

  • Multiple stakeholders could influence one commercial outcome.
  • Engagement occurred across marketing, events, referrals and direct sales.
  • Individual contacts could be connected to wider account relationships.
  • Not every attribution channel applied to every opportunity.

02

The attribution framework

The model evaluated recent and relevant interactions across a defined set of attribution channels. Each applicable channel received a starting weight based on its role in the commercial journey.

The framework recalculated those weights so that the final attribution across the opportunity always totalled 100%.

  • Marketing campaign engagement
  • Event and webinar participation
  • Direct sales engagement
  • Referral influence
  • Account relationships
  • Recent stakeholder activity

03

Salesforce architecture

Salesforce provided the central relationship and reporting layer. Leads, contacts, accounts, opportunities, campaigns and campaign members contributed different parts of the attribution picture.

Custom objects and structured relationships were used where standard records alone could not represent the full commercial journey.

  • Campaign members recorded marketing and event participation.
  • Opportunity relationships connected activity with commercial outcomes.
  • Account structures represented wider organisational influence.
  • Custom metadata kept attribution rules configurable.
  • Salesforce Flow supported repeatable calculations and record updates.

04

Weighted attribution logic

A channel only received attribution when supporting evidence showed that it had contributed to the opportunity. This prevented inactive or irrelevant channels from automatically receiving a share.

The remaining channel weights were then normalised. This preserved their relative importance while ensuring every completed calculation totalled exactly 100%.

  • Identify the channels applicable to the opportunity.
  • Retrieve the configured weight for each applicable channel.
  • Calculate the combined applicable weight.
  • Divide each channel weight by the applicable total.
  • Store the resulting percentage on the attribution record.

05

Governance and maintainability

The model was designed as a maintainable commercial framework rather than a group of hard-coded calculations. Attribution definitions and weights could be governed centrally and adjusted as organisational understanding developed.

Separating configuration from automation reduced the need to repeatedly edit Salesforce logic whenever a commercial rule changed.

  • Centralised attribution definitions
  • Configurable channel weights
  • Consistent calculation rules
  • Traceable engagement records
  • Repeatable quality checks

06

Reporting and commercial use

The resulting attribution records created a stronger foundation for evaluating campaign performance, event influence and the contribution of relationship-led sales activity.

The framework provided a consistent and transparent interpretation of the available commercial evidence rather than claiming that one calculation could perfectly explain every purchase.

  • Campaign and channel performance analysis
  • Opportunity-level influence reporting
  • Revenue contribution dashboards
  • Comparison of marketing and sales activity
  • Improved commercial planning and prioritisation

Outcomes

What the attribution framework enabled

More balanced attribution

Commercial influence could be distributed across multiple supported interactions rather than assigned entirely to one touchpoint.

Consistent calculations

Applicable channel weights were processed through one repeatable framework and normalised to 100%.

Configurable governance

Attribution rules could be managed centrally without embedding every assumption directly inside automation.

Stronger reporting

Salesforce data could support more useful analysis of campaign, event, referral and relationship influence.
