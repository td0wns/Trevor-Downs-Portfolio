Fresh Start in Education

Salesforce Multi-Touch Attribution and Revenue Reporting

A weighted Salesforce attribution model connecting recent email, event, sales and organisational engagement with enquiries, referrals, opportunities, placements and revenue reporting across a complex education-sector buying process.

Salesforce & CRM Automation Data & Analytics Commercial Strategy Marketing
Project overview

Built separate Salesforce Flow entry paths for Lead enquiries, Contact enquiries and Account-level referrals. The model used a three-week engagement window, Campaign Member activity, recognised organisational domains, MAT and Account context, returning-customer status and sequence-sensitive weighting, normalising applicable channel influence to 100% and storing the result for dashboards and revenue reports.

Key result Normalised applicable channel influence to 100% across events, mailshots, sales outreach and returning-customer relationships.

01

The commercial challenge

The organisation operated within a complex education-sector purchasing environment involving large buying committees, multiple stakeholders, high staff turnover and role-based email addresses. A person could engage with email marketing, attend an event or speak with sales, while a completely different person from the same organisation later submitted the website enquiry or referral.

Conventional person-level, first-touch or last-touch attribution could not reliably explain which activity had actually influenced the eventual outcome.

  • Large buying committees with several independently engaging stakeholders.
  • Enquiries or referrals frequently submitted by someone who had not personally engaged with earlier activity.
  • High staff turnover reducing the reliability of individual Lead and Contact records.
  • Frequent use of role-based email addresses across schools and local authorities.

02

Engagement tracking architecture

Built an engagement-tracking architecture using Salesforce Campaigns as indicators of recent, attributable behaviour. MCAE automation added prospects to engagement Campaigns when they opened or clicked qualifying email content, scheduled sales calls added Leads and Contacts to a separate Campaign, and event kiosk capture connected attendees with event-specific Campaigns.

A weekly Salesforce Flow purged and repopulated engagement Campaign membership using a three-week engagement window, so membership reflected recent commercially relevant interaction rather than permanent historical activity.

  • MCAE mailshot engagement Campaigns
  • Sales-call engagement Campaigns
  • Event kiosk and event Campaigns
  • Weekly purge and repopulation Flow
  • Three-week rolling engagement window

03

Three attribution Flow paths

The attribution model triggered through three separate Salesforce Flow entry paths: a Lead enquiry, a Contact enquiry, and an Account-level referral. Each path gathered active engagement Campaigns, MAT membership, Account context and related Lead or Contact activity before calculating a result.

The Account-level path evaluated engagement across the wider organisation rather than focusing on one person, looping through related contact data to identify the most relevant organisational domain.

  • Lead enquiry attribution Flow
  • Contact enquiry attribution Flow
  • Account-level referral attribution Flow
  • Related Lead and Contact engagement checks

04

Domain matching and organisational context

Where the submitting person did not use a government email address, the model used shared email-domain matching, backed by Custom Metadata Type reference data, to identify related Leads and Contacts within the same organisation. Domains ending in .gov.uk were excluded from this matching, as their broad usage did not support safe assumptions about interconnectedness.

Engagement by several related people within the same organisation could strengthen the score of the corresponding attribution channel, and Account status was factored in because recent and returning customers were more likely to purchase because of the existing relationship.

  • Shared email-domain matching
  • .gov.uk domain exclusion
  • Custom Metadata Type domain reference data
  • MAT and Account-status weighting

05

Sequence-sensitive weighting and normalisation

Custom weighting rules considered the sequence and relationship between touchpoints — for example, a preceding sales call reduced the relative influence assigned to later mailshot engagement, since direct sales interaction was likely to have been the stronger driver. Known purchasing patterns were also checked before assigning the final attribution.

Individual channel scores were summed into a total, each channel's proportional share was calculated, and the final attribution across events, returning-customer influence, mailshots and sales outreach was always normalised to 100%.

  • Touchpoint-sequence weighting
  • Purchasing-pattern checks
  • Total-score calculation
  • Proportional normalisation to 100%

06

Attribution Model object and reporting

Results were stored in a custom Attribution Model object, dynamically populated through Salesforce Flow to minimise manual data entry. The record held numerical attribution for each channel, lookups to the relevant Lead, Contact and converted Contact, the enquiring and referring Accounts, the related Opportunity, and referral-to-placement progression.

This preserved visibility across the full journey from initial engagement through enquiry, referral, Opportunity progression and placement, and fed a sales dashboard and multiple revenue reports.

  • Custom Attribution Model object
  • Lead, Contact and Account lookups
  • Opportunity and placement linkage
  • Sales dashboard and revenue reporting

Outcomes

What the attribution framework enabled

Buying-committee visibility

Enabled reporting to recognise organisational and buying-committee influence rather than crediting only the person who completed the final form.

Recent, relevant engagement

A three-week engagement window and weekly Campaign refresh kept attribution grounded in current activity rather than permanent historical engagement.

Safe organisational matching

Shared domain matching and .gov.uk exclusions connected related people without relying on unsafe assumptions about government-domain contacts.

Dynamic dashboards and reporting

Attribution results were populated automatically into a custom object, supporting a sales dashboard and multiple revenue reports without manual entry.
